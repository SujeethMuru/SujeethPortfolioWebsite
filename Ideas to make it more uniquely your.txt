  Ideas to make it more uniquely yours:

  1. Lead with a sharper identity
     Replace “I am a Computer Science student” with something more distinctive, such as:
     “Computer Science student building practical software, intelligent tools, and reliable IT solutions.”

  2. Turn projects into short case studies
     Feature your strongest three projects with:
      - The problem you identified
      - Your technical approach
      - One difficult decision or challenge
      - A measurable result
      - Screenshots or short demos

  3. Create an “Engineering Playground” section
     This could showcase smaller experiments involving local LLMs, cybersecurity labs, automation scripts, digital
     logic, or game development. That range is unusual and reflects your curiosity.

  4. Add an interactive project filter
     Let visitors filter projects by AI, Web, Cybersecurity, Games, and Hardware. This would make your varied experience
     feel intentional rather than disconnected.

  5. Show your current focus
     Add a compact “Currently” panel containing what you are learning, building, and looking for. For example: local AI
     agents, cybersecurity certifications, and software internships.

  6. Strengthen the experience section
     Add metrics and outcomes from your IT role, especially reliability improvements, number of systems supported,
     common problems solved, and technologies used.

  7. Use a personal visual motif
     A subtle terminal-inspired accent, circuit pattern, or “system status” component could connect your software,
     cybersecurity, IT, and digital-logic interests without making the site feel gimmicky.
